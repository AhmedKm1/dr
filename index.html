About DRAZA

Drawn from pain. Drawn for Gaza.

My name is Ahmed, and I never imagined I would be introducing myself this way. My fate placed me far from war studying abroad, alone, while everything I love was burning behind me.

I'm not just an artist. I'm a voice from the ashes.

I've lost 66 members of my family, and 35 of my friends. Now I fight every single day to save what's left: my mother, my little angel Eileen, my father, and my brother, before it's too late.

We once had a life full of color, pride, and dignity. We laughed, we lived. But that world is gone. Now, I'm left with memories, grief, and one tool that still listens to me: my art.

I was born with the gift of drawing and I started to draw 13 years ago in 2012. I won 1st place in a UNRWA art competition across Gaza in 2017. That award of oil colors and gouache paints lit something in me. Since then, I've poured my soul into painting. I sold artworks in galleries and shops, hoping someone might see me, support me, help my voice be heard. No one did.
So I created my own stage. I started from zero. And today, I've reached over 55 million views and over 200,000 followers on TikTok. But this isn't about fame. It's about saving lives. About telling the world what war really does to families.
Every artwork you see here is not just paint. It's grief, memory, and a prayer for one more sunrise with the people I love.

I don't want to live as an orphan. And I hope this story doesn't end the way so many of ours have.

Welcome to DRAZA — Drawn from pain. Drawn for Gaza.

Support & Donate

My name is Ahmed.

I'm a young Palestinian artist living abroad, and I feel ashamed that I was forced to ask for help here.

My family is still trapped in a war zone. We have lost 66 relatives and 35 friends. I am the only one outside who can provide for them.

I don't have connections. I don't have wealth. I don't have stability. But I have my art.

Medical Condition:

Progressive spinal curvature, degenerative disc disease, nerve compression, and chronic pain severely impact my daily life.

Drawing is the only thing I truly own. It's my voice… my strength… and the only path I have to support my family from afar.

This is not about comfort. This is survival.

Your support will help me:

  • Access physical therapy and healthcare
  • Support my mother, my brother, and my little sister Eileen in Gaza
  • Buy essential art tools and materials
  • Take courses and improve my skills
  • Build a stable artistic career
  • Keep hope alive

Every donation, no matter how small, brings me one step closer to building a future where my art becomes a lifeline.

Thank you for believing in my story.
